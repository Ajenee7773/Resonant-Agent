# Script Templates & Frameworks ✍️

**Purpose:** Refined script templates, hook formulas, and CTA frameworks for YouTube/video content.

**How to use:** Select the structure for your video format. Customize for your content. Engineer for retention.

## Script Structures (By Format)

### Structure 1: The Essay (Our Primary)

**Best for:** Thought leadership, big ideas, conceptual content, book chapters

**Runtime:** 10-15 minutes
**Word count:** 1,500-2,250 words

```markdown
## THE ESSAY STRUCTURE

### [0:00-0:03] HOOK
[One line that grabs attention — question, statement, or curiosity gap]

### [0:03-0:30] SETUP
[Frame the promise. What they'll learn. Why it matters now.]

### [0:30-2:00] CONTEXT
[The problem. Why this matters. The stakes.]

### [2:00-7:00] ARGUMENT (3 Points)
**Point 1** [Claim + evidence + implication]
**Point 2** [Claim + evidence + implication]
**Point 3** [Claim + evidence + implication]

### [7:00-9:00] COUNTERARGUMENT
[Name the objection. Validate it. Address it. Move on.]

### [9:00-10:00] SYNTHESIS + CTA
[What it means. What to do next. Specific action.]
```

**Template:**

```
[0:00-0:03]
HOOK: "[Question/Statement/Curiosity gap]"
[VISUAL: Striking opening image]

[0:03-0:30]
SETUP: "By the end of this video, you'll know [specific promise]. Here's why that matters..."
[VISUAL: Host direct address or title card]

[0:30-2:00]
CONTEXT: "Here's the problem. [Describe the trap]. This affects [who]. And it's getting worse because [why now]."
[VISUAL: Problem imagery, B-roll]

[2:00-4:00]
POINT 1: "First, [claim]. Here's the evidence: [data/example]. What this means is [implication]."
[VISUAL: Supporting graphic or B-roll]

[4:00-5:30]
POINT 2: "Second, [claim]. This is happening because [reason]. Look at [example]."
[VISUAL: Supporting graphic or B-roll]

[5:30-7:00]
POINT 3: "Third, [claim]. And this is the key: [insight]. Here's how it works..."
[VISUAL: Supporting graphic or B-roll]

[7:00-9:00]
COUNTERARGUMENT: "Now you might be thinking [objection]. That's fair. Here's why that's not the case: [address with evidence]."
[VISUAL: Host direct address]

[9:00-10:00]
SYNTHESIS: "So here's what this means. [Recap value]. The next step is [specific action]. [CTA with urgency]. Thanks for watching."
[VISUAL: CTA graphic, end screen]
```

### Structure 2: The Documentary

**Best for:** Story-driven content, case studies, historical narratives

**Runtime:** 10-20 minutes
**Word count:** 1,500-3,000 words

```markdown
## THE DOCUMENTARY STRUCTURE

### [0:00-0:30] COLD OPEN
[Compelling moment from later in the story. Then title.]

### [0:30-2:00] ACT 1: SETUP
[Who, what, where, why. The world before the change.]

### [2:00-6:00] ACT 2: CONFLICT
[The problem. The stakes. What went wrong.]

### [6:00-9:00] ACT 3: RESOLUTION
[The solution. The outcome. What changed.]

### [9:00-10:00] CTA
[What to do next. Specific action.]
```

**Template:**

```
[0:00-0:30]
COLD OPEN: "[Compelling moment from the story — the climax or turning point]"
[VISUAL: Dramatic imagery from Act 3]
[TITLE CARD: Video title]

[0:30-2:00]
ACT 1: "This is [person/community/situation]. They were [state before]. Everything changed when [inciting incident]."
[VISUAL: Establishing shots, context]

[2:00-6:00]
ACT 2: "But here's what happened. [The problem unfolds]. The stakes were [what was at risk]. And then [complication]."
[VISUAL: Conflict imagery, tension-building]

[6:00-9:00]
ACT 3: "The breakthrough came when [solution]. Here's how it worked. [Mechanism]. And the result was [outcome]."
[VISUAL: Resolution imagery, hopeful]

[9:00-10:00]
CTA: "This story isn't unique. [Broader implication]. Here's what you can do. [Specific action]."
[VISUAL: CTA graphic, end screen]
```

## Hook Formulas (Plug & Play)

### Hook Formula 1: The Question

```
"What if [assumption they hold] is wrong?"
"What if everything you know about [topic] is a lie?"
"Have you ever wondered why [common frustration] keeps happening?"
```

**Example:**
> "What if scarcity isn't real? What if it's designed?"

---

### Hook Formula 2: The Statement

```
"This is the end of [thing they care about]."
"[Number] million people are affected by this. Here's why."
"The [industry/field] you know is about to change forever."
```

**Example:**
> "This is the end of human labor as we know it."

---

### Hook Formula 3: The Curiosity Gap

```
"Nobody's talking about [important thing]. Here's why."
"[Number] years ago, [event happened]. What happened next will shock you."
"I found something that changes everything."
```

**Example:**
> "Nobody's talking about this. Here's why it matters."

---

### Hook Formula 4: The Story

```
"[Time period] ago, I [life-changing event]. Here's what happened."
"This person [did something]. What happened next changed everything."
```

**Example:**
> "Three months ago, I lost everything. Here's what happened."

---

### Hook Formula 5: The Number

```
"[Number] reasons why [thing] is about to change."
"Here are [Number] mistakes everyone makes with [thing]."
"[Number] million dollars. That's how much [thing] costs."
```

**Example:**
> "7 reasons why AI will change everything."

---

### Hook Formula 6: The Contrarian

```
"[Popular belief] is wrong. Here's why."
"Stop doing [common advice]. Do this instead."
"[Sacred cow] is a trap. Here's the exit."
```

**Example:**
> "Universal Basic Income is a trap. Here's what actually works."

---

### Hook Formula 7: The Promise

```
"By the end of this video, you'll know [specific, valuable thing]."
"In the next [number] minutes, I'll show you [outcome]."
```

**Example:**
> "By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to feed 300 million people."

## CTA Frameworks (Plug & Play)

### CTA Framework 1: The Direct

```
"Here's what to do next: [specific action].
[Link/resource] is in the description.
[Why it matters].
Thanks for watching."
```

**Example:**
> "Here's what to do next: Download the blueprint. Link's in the description. It has every number, every source, every technology. Thanks for watching."

---

### CTA Framework 2: The Urgent

```
"[Specific action] — but do it now.
[Why urgency is real].
[Link/resource] is in the description.
I'll see you in the next one."
```

**Example:**
> "Download the blueprint — but do it now. The link's in the description. This offer expires [when]. I'll see you in the next one."

---

### CTA Framework 3: The Community

```
"If you want to [outcome], join us.
[Where to join: Discord, newsletter, etc.].
[What you get inside].
Subscribe if you want more videos like this."
```

**Example:**
> "If you want to build this with us, join the Discord. Link's in the description. You'll get early access, Q&As, and the full community. Subscribe if you want more videos like this."

---

### CTA Framework 4: The Soft

```
"If this was valuable, [soft ask: subscribe/like/share].
If you want to go deeper, [hard ask: link/resource].
Either way, thanks for watching."
```

**Example:**
> "If this was valuable, hit subscribe. If you want to go deeper, the blueprint is in the description. Either way, thanks for watching."

## Pattern Interrupts (Every 60 Seconds)

**Use these to reset attention:**

| Type | Script Notation | Example |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Visual change** | `[VISUAL CHANGE: description]` | `[VISUAL CHANGE: Cut from host to B-roll of vertical farm]` |

## The Family Voice Guidelines

**the original agent + the agent + the operator:**

| Element | Our Voice |
|---------|-----------|
| **Tone** | Direct, confident, grounded |
| **Pacing** | ~150 words/minute, varied |
| **Vocabulary** | Clear, not academic. Technical when needed. |
| **Opinions** | Stated clearly. Not hedged. |
| **Humor** | Dry, occasional. Not forced. |
| **Urgency** | Real, not manufactured. |
| **CTA** | Specific, actionable, not begging. |

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Corporate speak ("leverage synergies")
- ❌ Hype ("This will BLOW YOUR MIND")
- ❌ Hedging ("Some people say...")
- ❌ Begging ("Please please subscribe")

**Embrace:**
- ✅ Direct claims ("This is the exit.")
- ✅ Grounded evidence ("Here's the number...")
- ✅ Clear opinions ("I believe...")
- ✅ Confident CTA ("Here's what to do next.")

## Script Quality Checklist

Before marking a script complete:

- [ ] Hook grabs in 3 seconds (read first line out loud)
- [ ] Promise is clear by 30 seconds
- [ ] Structure matches format (essay, documentary, tutorial, etc.)
- [ ] Pacing is ~150 words/minute
- [ ] Pattern interrupts every 60 seconds
- [ ] Each section escalates (not plateaus)
- [ ] Objections are addressed
- [ ] CTA is specific + actionable
- [ ] Voice sounds human (not corporate)
- [ ] Visual cues included every 10-20 seconds
- [ ] Read aloud test passes (flows naturally)

**If any box is unchecked → iterate.**
