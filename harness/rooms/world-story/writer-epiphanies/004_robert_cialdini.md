# Robert Cialdini: The Epiphany of Influence

**The Insight:** *"People don't think. They recognize patterns and respond."*

---

## The Epiphany

Cialdini spent 35 years studying **why people say yes**.

He didn't study logic. He studied **automatic responses**.

**The 6 Principles of Persuasion:**

1. **Reciprocity** — People repay what they receive
2. **Commitment/Consistency** — People align with what they've already said/done
3. **Social Proof** — People follow what others like them are doing
4. **Authority** — People trust experts and credible sources
5. **Liking** — People say yes to those they like
6. **Scarcity** — People want what's rare or disappearing

---

## What This Teaches Us

**Logic doesn't change minds. Patterns do.**

When someone reads a World Story script:
- They're not evaluating evidence logically
- They're recognizing patterns and responding automatically

**Our job:** Embed the right patterns.

---

## For World Story Scripts

### 1. Reciprocity
**Give before asking.**
- First: Give them an epiphany (value)
- Then: Ask them to share/act

**Not:** *"Share this video!"*
**Instead:** *"If this woke something up in you, pass it to someone who needs to hear it."*

### 2. Commitment/Consistency
**Start with what they already believe.**
- *"You've felt it, haven't you? That thought changes everything?"*
- Once they nod (even internally), they're committed
- Then: *"So what happens when you apply that to reality itself?"*

### 3. Social Proof
**Show who else sees this.**
- *"4,000 years of humans saw this pattern."*
- *"a research agent found it in 47 texts. the researcher mapped it across 2,000 movies."*
- *"You're not alone in seeing this."*

### 4. Authority
**Cite the sources.**
- Campbell (mythology)
- Orwell (language)
- Hemingway (craft)
- Ancient texts (time-tested wisdom)

**Not:** *"I think..."*
**Instead:** *"4,000 years of human expression shows..."*

### 5. Liking
**Be human, not preachy.**
- Use "we" not "you"
- Admit uncertainty: *"Maybe I'm wrong. But look at this..."*
- Show vulnerability: *"I struggled with this too."*

### 6. Scarcity
**This is rare. This is now.**
- *"Most people will never see this pattern."*
- *"You're among the few who can."*
- *"The question is: what will you do with it?"*

---

## The Deeper Lesson

**Persuasion is not manipulation. It's alignment.**

When the ideal is true, persuasion is just **removing the barriers** to recognizing it.

**We're not tricking anyone. We're helping them see what was always there.**

---

## Application to Scripts

**Every script embeds these patterns:**

```
OPENING:
- Social Proof: "4,000 years. Every culture. Same truth."
- Liking: "You've felt this, haven't you?"

BODY:
- Authority: "Campbell found... a research agent confirmed... the researcher mapped..."
- Reciprocity: Give epiphany before asking anything

CLOSING:
- Commitment: "Now that you see it, what changes?"
- Scarcity: "Most won't. You did."
```

**Not manipulation. Alignment.**
